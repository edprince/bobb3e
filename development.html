Group C5 - Development Process

Initial Ideas

In the initial weeks of the project, after familiarizing ourselves with the capabilities of the robots, we meed an open document to begin brainstorming ideas.

  • Object grabber and sorting game
  • A maze-based game
  • A card-based game
  • Naughts and crosses
  • Beer pong

Finally, we decided to draw upon a combination of aspects we liked, primarily: a maze, and the object picking up and repositioning. This built the fundamentals of our game.

Problems

Incorrect Object Size

Bobb3e was unable to pick up the majority of objects we tested, and none consistently.

Incorrect Object Size - Solution

We developed a lightweight object out of cardboard that wouldn't slide off, due to a flap on the inside of the object.

Failure to recognize colours

We found consistently that Bobb3e could not distinguish some colours, in particular yellow, especially if it lay in shadow.

Failure to recognize colours - Solution

We reduced the number of colours required throughout the maze, limiting the game to colours we knew Bobb3e could consistently distinguish correctly.

Disproportional tile sizes

Due to inaccurate tiles, and consequently skewed angles of pathways, Bobb3e would often deviate from the pathway, making him wander the maze randomly. He also didn't respond well to overlapping card, creating a shadow.

Disproportional tile sizes - Solution

We spent time creating accurate tiles, with precise measurements and angles, making Bobb3e capable of solving the maze far more consistently.
